# D.A.M.N.

### Greater Than The Sum

A DAMN stands for "Decentralized Autonomous Music Network". It represents all the pieces of web3 music tools and tech which combined create a network of open culture.&#x20;

A fun word to represent decentralized governance is "DAO" (decentralized autonomous organization).  This is also a made up fun name to sound smart and get the point across.

### Building A DAMN

"Building a DAMN" means creating and putting together many tools that empower artists to exist in web3.  This is imagination expanding technology, open for all.

One piece is [The Contract Wizard](../../create/tokens/the-contract-wizard/), the wizard who makes contracts.  This is a tool for everyone that makes it easier to create a record label.  Another part is [The Wizard's Library](../../create/tokens/the-contract-wizard/the-wizards-library.md), which is made possible by each individual artist who makes a contract.

[The Player](../../consume/universal-music-player/) is another essential part in building a DAMN.  The player lets you search any wallet or contract on Ethereum, and any file on Arweave.

[Serverless Playlists](../../curate/playlists/) are another core building block for decentralized music curation. So is the [Audio Uploader](../../create/files/audio-uploader/), with it's forward thinking tags that sets it apart from alternatives. [The Metadata Maker](../../create/the-metadata-maker/) is another important part in building a DAMN. The importance of thicc metadata cannot be understated.

### Strong and Open

Serverless Playlists allow users to create and share music collections that work across platforms, can't be censored, and are controlled only by the creator. The DAMN leverages these playlists to enable a music ecosystem where curation, discovery, and sharing are open, permanent, and not reliant on any single service or company.

This approach empowers users to upload, curate, and share, making music experiences more open and resilient.

### Curation Whether You Like It Or Not

If there's a closed platform that only plays the music NFTs from their site, or a library of a select group of approved platforms (the cool kids), and you're not one of the cool kids, don't worry.  The DAMN was built for you, not for the other guys.

Currently a lot of metadata and curation data is stored on centralized servers and APIs rather than on tags in the files and metadata in the tokens. This should be open, decentralized, and easy to understand.

We can incorporate the content from the closed platforms into the actual true vision of decentralized music, which is to build a DAMN. Not to build the crypto iTunes or Spotify or whatever. It's to build a DAMN. It doesn't matter that those other platforms won't play my music NFTs, though it's sad and has made me feel left out and inferior after years of building in public feeling like an outsider. It's not my fault that so many other platforms that use NFTs don't understand the fundamental point of NFTs. If they want to host their files on IPFS and then the files vanish once the company's funding dries up, that's not the point of building a DAMN. A DAMN naturally demands strong ideas for it's foundation.

Same with tags, it's hard to find other content with useful tags. It's even harder to find humans willing to collaborate on some Arweave tagging protocol for music. Instead of being distracted by all the missing information, you can gather the music you enjoy and tag it yourself.  This is what I mean about "whether they like it or not", collaboration will happen.  It would be better if everything was properly tagged, but if it isn't, we can tag it for them.  It will be a painful endeavour to tag everything, but the stuff you find that you like should be tagged to encourage the positive feedback loop of curation. There's a solution to preserve the provenance of the files and tokens while also wrapping them in more descriptive and functional metadata which enables discovery and a better listening experience.
